u · v = 0: the vectors are perpendicular. Neither points along the other at all.

The angle is acute, so u · v is positive: u leans the same way as v.

The geometric meaning

Drop a perpendicular from the tip of u onto the line through v. The signed length of that shadow is ‖u‖ cos θ. Multiply it by ‖v‖ and you get the dot product: u · v = ‖u‖‖v‖ cos θ. It is positive when the angle is acute, zero when the vectors are perpendicular, and negative when the angle is obtuse.

Following 3Blue1Brown's "Dot products and duality": taking u · v is the same as applying the row vector uᵀ, a 1×2 matrix that maps the plane to the number line, to v. The dot product hides a linear map.
